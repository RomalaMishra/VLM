# Accessibility Navigation Knowledge Base
# Source: COCO-80 classes filtered for indoor/outdoor navigation relevance
# Each entry = one retrieval chunk

## person
A person detected ahead means a human obstacle in your path. Slow down and announce your presence verbally. Maintain at least 1 meter of personal space. Do not walk directly toward a person; move to the side and proceed cautiously.

## bicycle
A bicycle in your path is a hard, narrow obstacle that can cause serious injury. Bicycles may be stationary or moving. Stop immediately and listen for movement. If stationary, move at least 1 meter around it. If moving, stay completely still until it passes.

## car
A car indicates you may be near a road or driveway. This is a high-danger zone. Stop walking immediately. Do not proceed until you can confirm the car is stationary and you are not in a traffic lane. Locate a sidewalk or safe edge before continuing.

## motorcycle
A motorcycle is similar to a bicycle but heavier and noisier. If you hear an engine, stay completely still. Motorcycles move fast and may not see pedestrians. Wait until the sound fades before moving. Avoid crossing any road area where motorcycles are detected.

## bus
A bus indicates a road or bus stop area. Buses are very large and have blind spots. Never walk behind or in front of a stopped bus without assistance. Stay on the pavement. If you need to board, wait at the designated stop area and ask for assistance.

## truck
A truck indicates a road, loading zone, or driveway. Trucks have large blind spots especially at the rear. Do not walk behind a truck. Stay well clear — at least 3 meters — and wait for the truck to stop its engine before moving past it.

## chair
A chair is a common low-level indoor obstacle. Chairs may be partially pulled out from a table and extend further into the path than expected. Move around it with a wide berth. Listen for scraping sounds that indicate someone is moving the chair.

## couch
A couch is a large, bulky indoor obstacle typically placed against a wall. It is usually stationary but may have cushions or items on the floor in front of it. Navigate around the full length of the couch. The area in front of a couch often has a coffee table nearby.

## dining table
A dining table is a waist-height obstacle with legs extending to the floor. The legs are the main trip hazard, not the tabletop. Move around the perimeter of the table entirely rather than trying to squeeze under or past it. Chairs are usually present around all four sides.

## potted plant
A potted plant is a floor-level or elevated obstacle. Floor plants are a direct trip hazard. The pot itself is hard and heavy. Move clearly around it. Elevated plants on stands may have the stand extending into your path at ankle level.

## bed
A bed is a large, room-dominating obstacle. Bed frames often extend close to the floor and are a shin-level hazard. The area around a bed is typically narrow. Navigate slowly and use a wall or furniture as a tactile guide. Corners of bed frames are sharp.

## refrigerator
A refrigerator indicates you are in a kitchen area. The refrigerator door swings outward and may be open. Do not walk directly in front of a refrigerator without checking if the door is open. Kitchen floors near refrigerators may be wet or slippery.

## oven
An oven is a kitchen hazard. If an oven is in use, surfaces may be extremely hot. Do not touch any surface near a detected oven. The oven door may be open and extends into the walkway. Kitchen areas often have confined spaces — move slowly.

## knife
A knife is a sharp object hazard. A knife detected on a surface means you are in a kitchen or dining area. Do not reach toward any surface in this area without confirming it is clear. Never pick up an unknown object in a kitchen without tactile confirmation of what it is.

## bottle
A bottle on the floor is a rolling trip hazard. If detected at floor level, stop and locate it before stepping forward. Bottles can roll unpredictably when nudged. In kitchen or bathroom areas, bottles may contain cleaning chemicals — do not handle without identification.
